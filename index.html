Joel Chavez

Howdy — I am a Ph.D. student in the Department of Government at Georgetown University, splitting my time between Washington DC & the West Texas desert.

With a focus on energy production markets, my research examines how state and federal policies engage areas with significant development to explore approaches local governments can take to limit negative externalities — such as those implied in the natural resource curse scholarship.

I am from Pecos, the namesake of a major river in the valley of the mountainous region in the Far West Texas desert. My hometown is quintessentially "Texan", being home of the world's first rodeo and situtated within the top area for oil and natural gas development in the Western Hemisphere.

The town is also nestled between Texas' two National Parks — Big Bend NP & Guadalupe Mountains NP (shown in the ensuing photos). Hence, my interest in the sphere that is natural resource policy. I am a former champion rodeo cowboy, a diehard Texas Longhorn, and a solid golf scramble partner.

A 2023 Pascal Ngoboka Grant recipient from the Southwestern Political Science Association, I previously served as the 2021 Senate Hispanic Research Council Luna Graduate Fellow — ultimately becoming a lead natural resource and energy policy analyst in the Texas Senate. I earned my Master of Public Service & Administration in the public policy analysis track from The Bush School of Government at Texas A&M, where my concentration was in Energy/Environment/Technology Policy & Management (EETPM). I earned my bachelors degree from The University of Texas - Austin.
